# The Kurt Vonnegut Guide to Peter Lynch Investing


### Welcome to the guide. 
It is a guide to the stock market, which is a large, invisible machine designed to move money from the nervous to the patient. It is also a machine designed to make common sense feel like a rare and exotic mineral.

Peter Lynch was a man who understood the machine. He was not a machine himself, which is why he was so good at it. He used things called "eyes" and "ears" to find companies that were actually doing something useful, rather than just shuffling paper in a tall building.

We have built this guide because human beings are prone to forgetting. We forget that a stock is a piece of a business, not a number on a flickering screen. We forget that the grocery store tells us more than the evening news. We have categorized these lessons into ten volumes, which you should read as if your financial future depended on it. Because, in a way, it does.

Here are the ten lessons you need to master if you want to avoid being ground up by the gears.
