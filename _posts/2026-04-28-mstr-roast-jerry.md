# What Is the Deal with MicroStrategy?

Have you looked at this company lately? They call themselves a "business intelligence" firm. That’s a great name. It sounds very important. You tell people at a party you work in "business intelligence," and they immediately think you’re a spy with a spreadsheet. But then you look at the books, and you realize the "intelligence" is just buying the same digital coin over and over again.

Imagine you own a dry cleaner. People bring you shirts, you clean the shirts, you make a little money. But instead of buying more hangers or better detergent, you take all the profit—and then you borrow a billion dollars—to buy vintage comic books. And you keep doing it. Eventually, nobody cares if the shirts are clean; they just want to know if the price of *Action Comics #1* went up. That is MSTR.

## The Proxy Paradox

Why do people buy this stock? You can buy the actual digital currency on your phone while you’re standing in line for a bagel. It’s right there! But no, investors want to buy a software company that owns the currency. It’s like paying a guy a fee to go to the store and buy you a gift card. Why am I paying you to hold the thing I could hold myself? 

And the premium! People are paying two, three times the value of the actual assets held by the company. It’s like going to a bank, seeing a hundred-dollar bill in the window, and saying, "I’ll give you three hundred dollars for that specific bill." Why? "Because I like the way you’ve framed it!" 

## The Leverage Loop

The best part is the borrowing. They issue debt to buy more. It’s the ultimate financial "double down." It’s like taking out a mortgage on your house to go to Vegas, and then using the winnings to buy a second house so you can mortgage *that* one too. As long as the table stays hot, you’re a genius. But if the dealer shows an Ace? Suddenly, that "business intelligence" starts looking a lot like a guy sweating over a roulette wheel at 4:00 AM.

At the end of the day, MSTR isn't a software company anymore. It’s a giant orange sail catching the wind of the crypto market. If the wind blows, you’re flying. If the wind stops... well, I hope you’re a good swimmer.
